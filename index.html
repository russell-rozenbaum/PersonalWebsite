Bio
I'm a senior at the University of Michigan, majoring in computer science
and minoring in music. My research interests lie largely in AI and type theory,
along with the intersect between them.

In particular, I am looking into the effects of high-level action commands used in
LLM-based agents developed for program synthesis.

In my freetime, I enjoy going to the gym, cooking, and making music (piano, beats, etc)!
Work
Winter 2025
Structure-Level Actions in LLM-based Coding Agents
I've been working with the Future of Programming Lab, led by Prof. Cyrus Omar, on the effects of high-level action commands used in LLM-based agents developed for program synthesis. Attached is my senior thesis on the topic, highlighting our work and current progress.


Summer 2024
Hazel's Exercise Mode
I started off with the Future of Programming Lab, led by Prof. Cyrus Omar, focusing on making Hazel's exercise mode title feature editable. This project continued into making all of Hazel's exercise mode features editable by instructors who may use Hazel as a learning tool for funtional programming.




Fall 2024 - Winter 2025
Kashmir Wildlife Foundation
As a part of KWF's AI team and BioAcoustics project, we focus on researching state-of-the-art techniques for audio data processing, bird classification, and general machine learning methods. We are developing upon this rapidly growing frontier, and designing, building, and testing an architecture to be deployed on edge devices for the use in counter-poaching efforts, specifically in Costa Rica's Osa Peninsula.
